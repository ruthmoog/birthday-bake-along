Make a Funfetti Vanilla Cake

We'll be making an un-iced funfetti vanilla cake. You'll need some tools and ingredients to make your cake. The ingredients below makes one 8-inch round cake.

The recipe is from Yolanda Gamp's book, 'How To Cake It'.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups | 130g | 5oz plain flour
  • 1¼ teaspoons baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon table salt
  • ½ cup | 115g | 4oz unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 cup | 200g | 7oz caster sugar
  • ½ teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs, at room temperature
  • ½ cup | 125ml | 8 fl.oz whole milk
  • ⅓cup | 75g rainbow sprinkles

What you'll need

  • medium mixing bowl + sieve + rubber spatula
  • electric beater (we're mixing for about 8 minutes)
  • 8-inch round cake tin, 3 inches deep
  • parchment paper (to line the tin) + pencil + scissors
  • vegetable shortening or unsalted butter or cooking spray
  • pairing knife or straight spatula
  • timer + cake tester or skewer (you can use a spear of dried spaghetti)
  • cooling rack

Italian Meringue Buttercream

The cake needs time to cool before icing, so we won't decorate the cake on the call. If you want to make your own icing, I am going to use this recipe at home. (You'll need a freestanding mixer or a lot of arm power).

Ingredients

  • 1¾ cups caster sugar
  • 8 large egg whites, at room temperature
  • 2 cups unsalted butter, cut into tablespoon-sized pieces, at room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Directions

  1. In a small saucepan, combine the sugar and half a cup of water. Place over medium heat and bring to a boil. Clip a candy thermometer to the side of the pan.
  2. While the sugar syrup is heating, put the egg whites in a bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the whisk attachment.
  3. When the syrup reaches 230°F|110°C begin to whip the egg whites on medium-high speed. Whip until the egg whites are stiff.
  4. When the syrup reaches 240°F|115°C, immediately remove the pan from the heat and, with the mixer running, pour the syrup into the egg whites in a very thin stream avoiding the whisk.
  5. Whip the meringue at high speed until thick and glossy and the bowl is no longer warm on the outside, about 8-12 mins.
  6. With the mixer running, add the butter a piece at a time, whipping until each piece is fully incorporated before adding the next. Scrape the sides down every now and then.
  7. After all the butter has been added, continue to whip until it's thick and smooth, 3-5 minutes. Beat in the vanilla.
  8. Use immediately, or transfer into an airtight container for up to 1 week in the fridge or up to 2 months in the freezer. Let it warm up to room temperature before using and stir to smooth it out.
